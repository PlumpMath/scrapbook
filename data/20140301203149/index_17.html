        In Emacs 24.2.4 (and probably in older versions), it is possible to list all supported encodings with the command list-character-sets.
        Then click on an encoding to display all its characters.
        The encoding 'unicode' is 3D and cannot be displayed. However, 'unicode-bmp' and 'unicode-sip' work fine and should be sufficient for anyone in the real world. That is very convenient to figure out your emacs fonts capabilities.

        You can also use the command list-charset-chars
