                                                                Jessica Hamrick Mod Karthik Puthraya • 6 months ago

                                                                If you have a region selected, M-; will comment it out. If you don't have a region selected, M-; will just add a comment at the end of the line. I am not sure if there is a shortcut for commenting out the current (unselected) line -- though there probably is. I usually just do C-a C-space C-e M-; (move to the beginning of the line, activate the mark move to the end of the line, comment).

                                                                        Russell Jessica Hamrick • 6 months ago

                                                                        You should check out evil-nerd-commenter, which does what you want and a few other things really well.

                                                                        To install, just copy the evil-nerd-commenter.el file from:
                                                                        https://github.com/redguardtoo...
                                                                        into ~/.emacs.d/evil-nerd-commenter.el

                                                                        Then put the following in your .emacs:

                                                                        (require 'evil-nerd-commenter)
                                                                        (global-set-key (kbd "M-;") 'evilnc-comment-or-uncomment-lines)
                                                                        (global-set-key (kbd "M-'") 'evilnc-copy-and-comment-lines)

                                                                                                                                                  Jessica Hamrick Mod King Don • 10 months ago

                                                                                                                                                  I'm not sure off the top of my head how to do it automatically (I think it depends on what your frame dimensions are -- if the frame is taller than it is wide, it splits horizontally; if it's wider than it is tall, it splits vertically). But, you can always do it manually: after doing "C-c !" to open up the interpreter, do "C-x 1" to remove all the splits, and then do either "C-x 3" to split it vertically or "C-x 2" to split it horizontally. You can then open up the buffer containing the interpreter in whichever window you want; the buffer is probably called something like *IPython*.
